Umm Kulthum (Arabic: أم كلثوم), born Fatima ʾIbrahim as-Sayyid al-Biltagi (فاطمة إبراهيم السيد البلتاجي), on an uncertain date (December 31, 1898, or May 4, 1904), died February 3, 1975), was an internationally renowned Egyptian singer, songwriter, and film actress active from the 1920s to the 1970s. She was given the honorific title Kawkab El Sharq (كوكب الشرق) "Star of the Orient". Umm Kulthum was known for her extraordinary vocal ability and style, and she was one of the greatest and most influential singers of the 20th century, where she has sold over 80 million records worldwide. Umm Kulthum is considered a national icon in her native Egypt and has been dubbed as The voice of Egypt and Egypt's fourth pyramid.
